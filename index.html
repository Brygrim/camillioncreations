Bio

Camille Grimshaw Artist & Educator

Camille Grimshaw teaches fine arts at American Academy of Innovation in South Jordan. Camille graduated from Westminster College with a bachelor's degree in Fine Arts and Secondary Education, minoring in Special Education, followed by a Master of Education with an ESL endorsement. She is currently working on her Master of Arts in Teaching Fine Arts at the University of Utah. She has been teaching art for over 20 years and is currently in her 10th year of teaching in the public school setting.

Outside of the classroom, Camille is an award-winning artist, including many best of shows and other top awards. She is an avid chalk artist and has been juried into several international chalk festivals. She is also a prolific volunteer, with hundreds of volunteer hours, many of which are directly related to sharing her art talents. Camille currently serves on the visual arts committees for the Harrington Center of the Arts and the Cultural Arts Society of West Jordan.

Camille is a mother of four children, Erin, Seth, Aven, and John, and is married to her high school sweetheart and biggest supporter, Bryan. Outside the art world, she loves traveling, hot-air ballooning, delicious food, and anything to do with the ocean. She is driven by her desire to help build a confident, creative community of artists and aesthetes who, in turn, can strengthen the arts for future generations.

Community Service

I have dedicated thousands of hours to community service throughout my career, with a significant portion devoted directly to the visual arts. I am very fortunate to make a living through the arts, but equally important is giving back to the arts. As a visual arts educator, creativity is a powerful way to build connection, confidence, and community. Through organizing exhibitions, leading workshops, mentoring students, and supporting collaborative art initiatives, I have worked to ensure that accessible artistic opportunities extend beyond the classroom and into the broader community. I also strive to help the other areas of fine arts whenever possible, recognizing the value of collaboration and unity among all creative disciplines.
